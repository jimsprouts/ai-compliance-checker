Document Version: 2.5

Document Owner: Chief Information Officer / Legal Department

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This policy establishes requirements for retaining and disposing of ACME
Corporation's business records and data assets in compliance with legal,
regulatory, and business requirements.

Purpose: Define what data to keep, how long to keep it, and how to dispose of it
Scope: All data types, all storage media, all business units
Compliance: Mandatory for all employees, contractors, and systems

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Purpose
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This policy ensures ACME Corporation:
✓ Complies with legal and regulatory retention requirements
✓ Preserves business-critical information
✓ Reduces storage costs by eliminating unnecessary data
✓ Mitigates legal and compliance risks
✓ Protects sensitive information through proper disposal
✓ Supports e-discovery and legal hold requirements

1.2 Scope
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This policy applies to all data in any format:

Electronic Data:
- Email and electronic communications
- Documents and spreadsheets
- Databases and structured data
- System logs and audit trails
- Backup and archive media
- Cloud-stored information
- Mobile device data
- Social media content

Physical Records:
- Paper documents
- Printed reports
- Contracts and agreements
- Personnel files
- Financial records
- Meeting minutes and notes

Other Media:
- Audio and video recordings
- Photographs and images
- Microfilm and microfiche
- Removable storage media (USB drives, optical discs)

3. RETENTION IMPLEMENTATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Active Data Retention
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 1: Active Storage (Online)
- Frequently accessed data
- Stored on primary storage systems
- Immediately available
- Backed up according to backup policy
- Duration: Per retention schedule

Phase 2: Near-Line Storage
- Infrequently accessed data
- Moved to lower-cost storage
- Available within hours
- Reduced backup frequency
- Duration: Varies by data type

Phase 3: Archival Storage (Offline)
- Rarely accessed data
- Long-term retention requirement
- Retrieval may take days
- Stored on tape or cold storage
- Duration: Remainder of retention period

3.2 Email Retention
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Automatic Archiving:
- All email automatically archived after 90 days
- Archive searchable by users
- Retention applied based on classification
- Legal hold capability enabled

Retention Categories:
- General Business Email: 3 years
- Executive Email: 7 years
- Legal/Compliance Email: 7 years
- HR Email: 7 years
- Finance Email: 7 years

User Responsibilities:
- Do not delete emails subject to legal hold
- Classify important emails appropriately
- Do not use personal email for business

3.3 Document Management
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SharePoint/Document Management System:
- Automatic retention labels applied
- Workflows for approval and review
- Version control enabled
- Audit trail maintained
- Automatic deletion after retention period

File Shares:
- Retention policies applied automatically
- Quarterly cleanup reminders
- Data owner review required
- Orphaned data identified and removed

3.4 Database Retention
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Operational Databases:
- Active transactions: Online
- Historical transactions: Archived after fiscal year
- Retention per data classification
- Archive databases for long-term storage

Data Warehouse:
- Aggregated data retained longer
- Detail data summarized over time
- Archive older detail data
- Maintain for trend analysis

4. DATA DISPOSAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 Electronic Data Disposal
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Secure Deletion Methods:

Hard Drives and SSDs:
✓ DoD 5220.22-M (7-pass overwrite) for HDDs
✓ Cryptographic erasure for encrypted drives
✓ ATA Secure Erase for SSDs
✓ Physical destruction for highly sensitive data

Approved Tools:
- DBAN (Darik's Boot and Nuke)
- Blancco Drive Eraser
- Manufacturer utilities (for SSDs)
- Degausser (for magnetic media)

Disposal Process:
1. Identify data for disposal
2. Verify retention period expired
3. Check for legal holds
4. Execute approved deletion method
5. Generate certificate of destruction
6. Log disposal in asset management system

4.2 Physical Media Disposal
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Paper Records:
✓ Cross-cut shredding (minimum P-4 security level)
✓ Locked shred bins in secure areas
✓ Certified destruction service
✓ Certificate of destruction obtained

Backup Tapes:
✓ Degaussing (for magnetic tapes)
✓ Physical destruction (shredding/crushing)
✓ Certificate of destruction

Optical Media (CDs/DVDs):
✓ Physical destruction (shredding)
✓ Incineration for highly sensitive data

4.3 Device Disposal
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Computers and Laptops:
1. Full disk encryption verification
2. Cryptographic key destruction
3. Multiple-pass overwrite OR physical destruction
4. Asset tag removal
5. Disposal through certified e-waste recycler

Mobile Devices:
1. Factory reset (multiple times)
2. Encryption key wipe
3. Physical destruction of storage chips
4. Certificate of destruction

Printers and Copiers:
1. Remove and destroy hard drives
2. Clear memory and settings
3. Dispose through certified recycler

4.4 Cloud Data Disposal
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SaaS Applications:
✓ Request data deletion from vendor
✓ Verify deletion completed
✓ Obtain certificate of deletion
✓ Review data processing agreement

Cloud Storage (AWS/Azure/GCP):
✓ Delete objects/files
✓ Delete encryption keys (cryptographic erasure)
✓ Delete backups and snapshots
✓ Verify deletion in all regions
✓ Document deletion in change log

4.5 Disposal Verification
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
All data disposal must include:
□ Disposal request form completed
□ Approval from data owner
□ Verification retention period expired
□ Legal hold check performed
□ Approved method used
□ Certificate of destruction obtained
□ Disposal logged in tracking system
□ Annual audit of disposal processes

5. LEGAL HOLD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 Legal Hold Process
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When Legal Hold Initiated:
1. Legal department issues legal hold notice
2. IT freezes automatic deletion
3. Affected custodians notified
4. Data preservation begins immediately
5. Monitoring and compliance tracking

Legal Hold Duration:
- Until litigation/investigation concluded
- Release authorized by Legal department only
- Data restored to normal retention after release

5.2 Legal Hold Scope
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
May Include:
✓ Email communications
✓ Documents and files
✓ Database records
✓ Instant messages
✓ Social media content
✓ System logs
✓ Backup tapes
✓ Any potentially relevant data

5.3 Custodian Responsibilities
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Employees under legal hold must:
- Preserve all relevant data
- Not delete any potentially relevant information
- Notify IT of any issues
- Acknowledge legal hold notice
- Attend training if required

7. EXCEPTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Exception Process:
1. Submit written request
2. Business justification required
3. Legal review and approval
4. Documented in exception register
5. Annual review required
6. Compensating controls may be required

Valid Exception Examples:
- Ongoing litigation (legal hold supersedes retention)
- Regulatory investigation (extended retention)
- Business-critical historical data (with approvals)
- Research and development data (with justification)
