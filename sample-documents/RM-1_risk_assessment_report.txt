1.3 Risk Calculation Method
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Risk Level = Likelihood × Impact

Likelihood Scale (1-5):
1 = Rare (< 5% annual probability)
2 = Unlikely (5-25% annual probability)
3 = Possible (25-50% annual probability)
4 = Likely (50-75% annual probability)
5 = Almost Certain (> 75% annual probability)

Impact Scale (1-5):
1 = Negligible (< $10K loss, minimal disruption)
2 = Minor ($10K-$100K loss, < 1 day disruption)
3 = Moderate ($100K-$500K loss, 1-3 days disruption)
4 = Major ($500K-$5M loss, 3-7 days disruption)
5 = Severe (> $5M loss, > 7 days disruption or catastrophic impact)

Risk Rating Matrix:
Impact →    1         2         3         4         5
Likelihood ↓
5          Medium    High      High      Critical  Critical
4          Low       Medium    High      High      Critical
3          Low       Medium    Medium    High      High
2          Low       Low       Medium    Medium    High
1          Low       Low       Low       Medium    Medium

2. CRITICAL RISKS (RISK SCORE 20-25)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RISK #1: Insufficient Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Coverage
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Risk ID: CR-2025-001
Category: Access Control
Current Status: OPEN

Threat: Account compromise through credential theft or phishing
Vulnerability: Only 60% of user accounts have MFA enabled
Asset at Risk: User accounts, corporate systems, sensitive data

Likelihood: 4 (Likely)
Impact: 5 (Severe - potential for widespread data breach)
Risk Score: 20 (CRITICAL)

Current Controls:
- MFA available via Microsoft Authenticator
- Enforced for VPN access
- Enforced for administrators

Gap Analysis:
- 40% of standard user accounts lack MFA
- No MFA for some legacy applications
- Inconsistent enforcement across systems

Business Impact:
- Potential data breach affecting 10,000+ customer records
- Regulatory fines (GDPR, SOX)
- Reputation damage
- Estimated financial impact: $2M - $10M

Recommended Treatment:
□ Phase 1 (30 days): Enforce MFA for all cloud applications
□ Phase 2 (60 days): Enforce MFA for all VPN and remote access
□ Phase 3 (90 days): Enforce MFA for all user accounts
□ Phase 4 (120 days): Implement MFA for legacy applications

Owner: IT Security Director
Target Completion: April 30, 2025
Budget Required: $50,000 (licensing + implementation)

RISK #2: Unpatched Critical Vulnerabilities in Production Systems
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Risk ID: CR-2025-002
Category: Vulnerability Management
Current Status: IN PROGRESS

Threat: Exploitation of known vulnerabilities by threat actors
Vulnerability: 15 production servers with critical patches pending > 30 days
Asset at Risk: Production servers, customer data, business operations

Likelihood: 5 (Almost Certain - actively exploited in the wild)
Impact: 4 (Major - system compromise, data exposure)
Risk Score: 20 (CRITICAL)

Current Controls:
- Vulnerability scanning (weekly)
- Patch management process
- Change management approvals

Gap Analysis:
- Patch testing delays application
- Change freeze windows limit patching
- Some systems require downtime for patching
- Resource constraints in IT operations

Affected Systems:
- 8 Windows servers (critical security updates pending)
- 4 Linux servers (kernel vulnerabilities)
- 3 database servers (SQL Server vulnerabilities)

Known Exploits:
- CVE-2024-XXXXX (actively exploited, CVSS 9.8)
- CVE-2024-YYYYY (public exploit available, CVSS 9.1)

Business Impact:
- Potential system compromise
- Service disruption (24-48 hours)
- Data breach possibility
- Estimated financial impact: $1M - $5M

Recommended Treatment:
□ Emergency patching of critical vulnerabilities (within 7 days)
□ Implement expedited patch testing process
□ Schedule maintenance windows for remaining patches
□ Consider compensating controls (network segmentation, IPS rules)

Owner: IT Operations Manager
Target Completion: February 15, 2025
Budget Required: $0 (resources reallocated)

RISK #3: Inadequate Backup and Disaster Recovery Testing
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Risk ID: CR-2025-003
Category: Business Continuity
Current Status: OPEN

Threat: Ransomware attack or natural disaster causing data loss
Vulnerability: Backup recovery not tested for 18 months
Asset at Risk: All business data, system availability

Likelihood: 4 (Likely - ransomware attacks increasing)
Impact: 5 (Severe - potential permanent data loss)
Risk Score: 20 (CRITICAL)

Current Controls:
- Daily backups to on-site storage
- Weekly backups to cloud storage
- Backup monitoring and alerts
- Disaster recovery plan documented

Gap Analysis:
- Backup recovery not tested since June 2023
- Recovery time objectives (RTO) not validated
- No ransomware-specific recovery plan
- Backup integrity verification incomplete

Last Test Results (June 2023):
- 3 out of 5 systems successfully restored
- 2 systems had backup corruption issues
- Average recovery time: 12 hours (exceeds 4-hour RTO)

Business Impact:
- Potential data loss (up to 24 hours)
- Extended downtime (days vs hours)
- Business operations halted
- Customer impact and revenue loss
- Estimated financial impact: $5M - $15M

Recommended Treatment:
□ Conduct full DR test within 30 days
□ Implement monthly backup recovery tests (sample systems)
□ Deploy immutable backups (ransomware protection)
□ Document and update DR procedures
□ Train DR team on recovery procedures

Owner: Infrastructure Manager
Target Completion: March 15, 2025
Budget Required: $75,000 (immutable backup solution)
