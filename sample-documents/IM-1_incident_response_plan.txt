2. INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION

2.1 Severity Levels

CRITICAL (Priority 1)
- Complete system outage affecting business operations
- Confirmed data breach with sensitive information compromised
- Ransomware or widespread malware infection
- Active cyber attack in progress
- Response Time: Immediate (within 15 minutes)

HIGH (Priority 2)
- Partial system outage affecting key services
- Suspected unauthorized access to critical systems
- Significant security control failure
- Response Time: Within 1 hour

MEDIUM (Priority 3)
- Limited service degradation
- Failed intrusion attempts
- Minor policy violations
- Response Time: Within 4 hours

LOW (Priority 4)
- Isolated issues with minimal impact
- Potential security vulnerabilities identified
- Response Time: Within 24 hours

3. INCIDENT RESPONSE PHASES

3.1 PREPARATION
- Maintain updated incident response toolkit
- Regular team training and tabletop exercises (quarterly)
- Keep contact lists current
- Maintain backup and recovery procedures
- Document all systems and network diagrams

3.2 IDENTIFICATION
- Monitor security alerts from SIEM, IDS/IPS, and antivirus systems
- Analyze anomalous behavior or performance issues
- Review user reports of suspicious activity
- Document initial findings and assign severity level
- Activate incident response team if severity warrants

3.3 CONTAINMENT
Short-term Containment:
- Isolate affected systems from network
- Disable compromised user accounts
- Block malicious IP addresses or domains
- Preserve evidence for forensic analysis

Long-term Containment:
- Apply security patches
- Rebuild compromised systems
- Implement additional monitoring
- Review and strengthen access controls

3.4 ERADICATION
- Remove malware or malicious code
- Close vulnerability that allowed incident
- Reset compromised passwords
- Review and remove unauthorized access
- Verify complete removal of threat

3.5 RECOVERY
- Restore systems from clean backups
- Gradually return systems to production
- Monitor for signs of re-infection
- Verify business operations are normal
- Enhanced monitoring for 30 days post-incident

3.6 LESSONS LEARNED
- Conduct post-incident review within 7 days
- Document timeline and actions taken
- Identify what worked and what didn't
- Update incident response procedures
- Implement preventive measures
- Share findings with stakeholders

4. INCIDENT LOGGING AND DOCUMENTATION

All incidents must be logged with the following information:
- Date and time of detection
- Date and time of occurrence (if different)
- Person who reported the incident
- Nature and severity of the incident
- Systems and data affected
- Actions taken
- Outcome and resolution
- Lessons learned

Incident Log Location: SharePoint/Security/Incident_Logs
Retention Period: 7 years

6. SPECIFIC INCIDENT PROCEDURES

6.1 Data Breach Response
1. Confirm breach and scope
2. Contain breach immediately
3. Notify Legal and CISO
4. Preserve forensic evidence
5. Begin regulatory notification process
6. Prepare customer notification
7. Engage cyber insurance
8. Monitor for identity theft

6.2 Ransomware Response
1. Isolate affected systems immediately
2. Do NOT pay ransom without executive approval
3. Identify ransomware variant
4. Check for available decryption tools
5. Restore from clean backups
6. Report to law enforcement
7. Implement enhanced email filtering

6.3 Malware Outbreak
1. Identify patient zero
2. Quarantine infected systems
3. Update antivirus signatures
4. Scan all systems
5. Remove malware
6. Patch vulnerabilities
7. Monitor for reinfection
